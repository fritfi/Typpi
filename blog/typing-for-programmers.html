Typing for Programmers: Speed vs. Thought

Does typing speed actually matter when your bottleneck is thinking?

A common argument in the software development world is that typing speed doesn't matter because "programmers spend more time thinking than typing." While the second part is true, the conclusion is flawed.

The "Brain-to-Code" Latency

Think of your typing speed as the bandwidth between your brain and the computer. Even if you spend 90% of your time thinking, when you finally have the solution, you want to get it into the editor as quickly as possible.

If you type slowly, you risk losing your train of thought. High latency interrupts your "flow state."

Special Characters are the Enemy

Standard typing tests use prose. Programming uses symbols: `{} [] () ; : " ' ! = + - * /`.

Many developers can type 80 WPM in English but drop to 40 WPM when writing code because they have to hunt for these symbols. This is where targeted practice comes in.

How to Practice Coding Syntax

Don't just practice with "The quick brown fox." Use Typpi to practice with actual code snippets.

  • Paste a function: Take a complex function from your codebase and paste it into Typpi.
  • Turn on Punctuation: Make sure the punctuation toggle is ON.
  • Focus on the symbols: Pay attention to which finger you use for `{` or `_`. Is it consistent?

Conclusion

You don't need to type 150 WPM to be a great developer. But if you can type 60-80 WPM comfortably including special characters, you remove a significant layer of friction between your ideas and your reality.
